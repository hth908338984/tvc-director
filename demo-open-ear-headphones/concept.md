# 15s TVC Demo: AeroLoop Open-Ear Headphones

## Demo Assumptions

- Product: concept open-ear sport headphones, no product reference image.
- Duration: 15 seconds.
- Format: 16:9 horizontal.
- Visual style: D. high-end CG engine look, close to real product cinematography.
- Product integration: cinematic product breakdown.
- Output target: Nano Banana Pro for product assets and storyboard keyframes; Seedance / Jimeng Multi-Phase for video.

## Creative Direction

### Concept: Air Without Weight

A pair of titanium open-ear sport headphones appears from darkness as if assembled by air pressure and sound waves. The film never leaves the product world: every second is about structure, lightness, sweat resistance, and secure fit, expressed through material macro shots, floating component separation, and airflow visualization.

## Core Selling Points

- Lightweight titanium memory frame.
- Open-ear comfort for outdoor running and cycling.
- Water-resistant acoustic module.

## Brand Tone

Precise, athletic, quiet, premium, technical.

## Narrative Model

Model B: cinematic product breakdown.

The product has a clear structure and material story, so a pure product micro-film is more stable for AI generation than a lifestyle scene with full human movement. This is the safest quick-demo path because it reduces face, body, and environment consistency problems.

## Visual System

- Color arc: deep graphite black -> cool titanium silver -> electric cyan accent.
- Lighting: low-key studio, narrow rim light, moving cyan scan light.
- Visual motif: airflow ribbons and sound-wave rings.
- End frame: product floating in calm hero pose with clean logo and slogan space.

## 15s Structure

| Time | Beat | Visual Function |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0-3s | Awakening | Product silhouette revealed by rim light |
| 3-7s | Breakdown | Ear hook, speaker pod, battery core, and control module separate in slow motion |
| 7-11s | Function | Sound waves and airflow visualize open-ear listening and outdoor awareness |
| 11-13s | Reassembly | Components snap back into a stable lightweight form |
| 13-15s | End Frame | Product hero pose with reserved brand text space |

## AI Notes

- Avoid people in this fast demo. Human motion and wearable consistency are expensive to control.
- Use concept-product prompt path because no product reference image is assumed.
- Keep all product frames in a pure studio world so the multi-view product asset can anchor every later generation.
